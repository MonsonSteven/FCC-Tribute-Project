Django Reinhardt

The greatest guitarist you've never heard.

Here's a timeline of Django's life:

  • 1910 - Born in Liberchies, Belgium
  • 1922 - Is gifted a violin and learns to play.
  • 1928 - Is severely injured in a house fire, causing lifetime damage and maiming his left hand. Django must relearn guitar completely, with only 2 functional fingers.
  • 1929 - Celebrates the birth of his first son, Henri "Lousson" Reinhardt.
  • 1934 - Meets the violinist Stephane Grappelli. The two would form "the Quintet of The Hot Club of Paris", a revolutionary string jazz quintet, vastly different from the Jazz norms of the time period.
  • 1935-1938 - Quintet makes numerous recordings, and Django collaborates with numerous Jazz legends, including Dizzy Gillespie and Louis Armstrong. This culminates with his 1938 performance at London's Kilburn State and the London Palladium.
  • 1939-1945 - Nazi invasion of France seperates Django and Grappelli, with the guitarist staying in France and the violinist fleeing to England. They wouldn't reunite for years.
  • 1946 - Rejoins Grappelli in England, with plans for an American tour.
  • 1946 - While on American tour, meets Duke Ellington and becomes lifelong friends, performs in his Orchestra, also.
  • 1947 - Unable to find work in California, Reinhardt return to Paris.
  • 1948 -Struggles to find place again musically, begins no-showing events and is viewed as unreliable.
  • 1949 - Recruits 3 Italian musicians and records around 60 tracks with a new sound and electric guitar replacing his signature Selmer.
  • 1950 - Joins and tours with Benny Goodman, touring in the U.S.
  • 1951 - Retires to Samois-sur-Seine, near Fountainebleau. Continues to play locally.
  • 1953 - Dies of a brain hemorrhage while walking home from perfomance. Aged 43
  • 1970s-present - Is rediscovered by generations of guitarists, is considered a pioneer and technical prodigy.
